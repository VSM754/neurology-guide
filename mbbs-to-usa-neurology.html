Step‑by‑Step Guide: MBBS to Neurology in the USA

A complete roadmap for Indian MBBS graduates who want to become neurologists in the United States.

Overview

Many Indian MBBS graduates dream of practicing neurology in the United States. This guide explains the exact steps required — from USMLE exams to residency, visas, and fellowship.

Step 1: Complete MBBS in India

  • Finish the 5.5‑year MBBS program (including internship).
  • Maintain strong academic performance.
  • Participate in research or clinical electives if possible.

Step 2: Prepare for USMLE Exams

The U.S. requires all international doctors to pass the USMLE exams.

USMLE Steps

  • Step 1: Basic sciences (very important for residency applications).
  • Step 2 CK: Clinical knowledge.
  • OET: English proficiency exam required for ECFMG certification.

When to Take USMLE?

  • Step 1 → During MBBS or after internship.
  • Step 2 CK → After internship.

Step 3: Get ECFMG Certification

ECFMG (Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates) certifies that your MBBS degree is valid for U.S. training.

  • Pass USMLE Step 1.
  • Pass USMLE Step 2 CK.
  • Pass OET.
  • Verify MBBS degree through ECFMG.

Step 4: Build a Strong Residency Application

Neurology is moderately competitive. A strong application includes:

  • High USMLE scores.
  • U.S. clinical experience (observerships, externships).
  • Letters of recommendation from U.S. doctors.
  • Research experience (neurology preferred).
  • Strong personal statement.

Step 5: Apply for Residency (Neurology)

Applications are submitted through ERAS (Electronic Residency Application Service).

Neurology Residency Structure

  • 1 year Internal Medicine (Preliminary Year)
  • 3 years Neurology Residency

Total: 4 years

Step 6: Visa Options

J‑1 Visa (Most Common)

  • Easier to obtain.
  • Requires 2‑year home return rule (can be waived).

H‑1B Visa

  • Harder to get.
  • Some programs do not sponsor H‑1B.
  • No home return rule.

Step 7: Fellowship (Optional but Common)

After neurology residency, many doctors pursue a fellowship:

  • Stroke
  • Epilepsy
  • Movement Disorders
  • Neurocritical Care
  • Neuromuscular Medicine
  • Sleep Medicine

Duration: 1–3 years

Step 8: Become a Board‑Certified Neurologist

  • Pass the American Board of Psychiatry and Neurology (ABPN) exam.
  • Start practicing as a neurologist in the U.S.

Timeline Summary

MBBS → Neurologist in the USA
Stage Duration
MBBS 5.5 years
USMLE + ECFMG 1–2 years
Residency (Neurology) 4 years
Fellowship (Optional) 1–3 years
Total 11–14 years

Final Notes

  • The U.S. pathway is long but offers excellent training and high salaries.
  • Strong USMLE scores and U.S. clinical experience are critical.
  • Many Indian MBBS graduates successfully match into neurology every year.
