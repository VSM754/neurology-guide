Green Card vs No Green Card: Education Impact

How immigration status shapes tuition, financial aid, and career pathways.

Executive Summary

Immigration status significantly impacts tuition costs, financial aid, and access to medical schools. A green card simplifies the path to a neurology career.

High School Impact

  • No difference in school access
  • Same academic and extracurricular opportunities

College Admissions

With Green Card

  • Domestic student status
  • Lower tuition (possible in-state)
  • Eligible for financial aid
  • More scholarships

Without Green Card

  • International status
  • Higher tuition
  • Limited aid
  • Fewer scholarships

Tuition Comparison

Type Annual Tuition
In-State $10k–$15k
Out-of-State $25k–$35k
International $30k–$50k

Medical School Access

  • Green Card: Access to all U.S. schools
  • No Green Card: Limited schools

Residency Path

  • Green Card: No visa required
  • No Green Card: Visa required

Age 21 Risk

Without a green card, students must switch visas at 21, increasing cost and limiting opportunities.

Summary

Category Green Card No Green Card
Tuition Lower Higher
Financial Aid Available Not available
Medical Access Full Limited
Career Path Smoother More complex

Final Recommendation

Obtaining a green card before age 21 provides major long-term advantages and simplifies the neurology career path.
