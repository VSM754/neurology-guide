Green Card vs No Green Card

Impact on a student’s education and neurology career path.

Executive Summary

Immigration status plays a major role in education, financial aid, and medical career opportunities. Having a green card significantly simplifies the journey to becoming a neurologist.

High School Impact

  • No difference in access to public schools
  • Same AP, honors, and extracurricular opportunities

College Admissions

With Green Card

  • Domestic student status
  • Lower tuition (in-state possible)
  • Eligible for financial aid
  • More scholarships

Without Green Card

  • International student status
  • Higher tuition
  • No federal aid
  • Limited scholarships

Tuition Comparison

Type Annual Tuition
In-State $10k–$15k
Out-of-State $25k–$35k
International $30k–$50k

Medical School Access

  • Green Card: All U.S. medical schools
  • No Green Card: Limited (~40 schools)

Residency & Career Path

  • Green Card: No visa issues
  • No Green Card: Visa required (J‑1 / H‑1B)

Key Risk (Age 21 Rule)

H‑4 visa holders must switch to F‑1 at age 21 if no green card is obtained, which increases cost and reduces opportunities.

Summary

Category Green Card No Green Card
Tuition Lower Higher
Financial Aid Available Not available
Medical Schools All Limited
Career Path Smooth More barriers

Final Recommendation

Obtaining a green card before age 21 provides major advantages and simplifies the path to a neurology career.
